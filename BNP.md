# B-type Natriuretic Peptide (BNP)

**Normal Range:**  
- **< 100 pg/mL** → Normal (no heart failure)  
- **100 – 300 pg/mL** → Possible heart strain, further testing needed  
- **> 300 pg/mL** → Suggestive of heart failure (severity increases with levels)  


**Clinical Significance:**  
BNP is a hormone released from the heart’s ventricles in response to **increased wall stress, fluid overload, or heart failure**.  
- Low BNP: rules out heart failure.  
- High BNP: indicates cardiac stress or decompensated heart failure.  
- BNP helps in **differentiating cardiac vs. pulmonary causes** of breathlessness.  


**Future Diseases Associated with Abnormal Levels:**  
- **Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)**  
- **Left ventricular dysfunction**  
- **Pulmonary hypertension**  
- **Renal dysfunction (can falsely elevate BNP)**  
- **Acute coronary syndrome**  


**Lifestyle Recommendations:**  
- Maintain **healthy blood pressure** and **cholesterol levels**  
- Follow a **low-salt diet** to reduce fluid overload  
- Regular **exercise and weight control**  
- Take medications (diuretics, ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers) as prescribed  
- Avoid smoking and excess alcohol  
- Routine **cardiology follow-up** if elevated

# Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)

**Normal Range (Reference Intervals):**  
- Adults: **44 – 147 IU/L**  
- Children/Adolescents: **Up to 500 IU/L** (higher due to bone growth)  
- Pregnancy: Mildly elevated (placental isoenzyme)  


---

**Clinical Significance:**  
Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) is an enzyme found mainly in the **liver, bile ducts, and bone**.  
- Elevated ALP may indicate **liver disease, bile duct obstruction, or bone disorders**.  
- Low ALP is rare but may be linked to **malnutrition, hypothyroidism, or genetic hypophosphatasia**.  

---

**Future Diseases Associated with Abnormal Levels:**  
- **Liver disease** (hepatitis, cirrhosis, liver cancer)  
- **Biliary obstruction** (gallstones, cholestasis)  
- **Bone disorders** (Paget’s disease, osteomalacia, bone tumors)  
- **Vitamin D deficiency**  
- **Bone growth (children, adolescents, pregnancy – usually benign cause)**  

---

**Lifestyle Recommendations:**  
- Ensure **adequate calcium and vitamin D** intake.  
- Limit **alcohol consumption** (to protect liver).  
- Maintain **healthy weight** to reduce fatty liver disease risk.  
- For abnormal results: follow up with **liver function tests (LFTs)** and **bone scans** as guided by a physician.

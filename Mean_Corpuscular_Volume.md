# Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)

**Normal Range (Reference Intervals):**  
- **Adults:** 80 – 100 fL  
- **Children (6–12 years):** 77 – 95 fL  
- **Infants (1–12 months):** 70 – 86 fL  
- **Newborns (0–1 month):** 96 – 108 fL  

**Clinical Significance:**  
MCV measures the average size of red blood cells (RBCs). It helps classify anemia into three main types:  
- **Microcytic (< 80 fL):** Commonly iron deficiency or thalassemia.  
- **Normocytic (80 – 100 fL):** Often due to chronic disease, kidney disease, or acute blood loss.  
- **Macrocytic (> 100 fL):** Often vitamin B12 or folate deficiency, alcohol use, or bone marrow disorders.  

**Future Diseases & Risks of Abnormal Levels:**  
- **Low MCV (Microcytosis):**  
  - Iron deficiency anemia  
  - Thalassemia  
  - Chronic blood loss  
  - Lead poisoning  

- **High MCV (Macrocytosis):**  
  - Vitamin B12 deficiency  
  - Folate deficiency  
  - Alcoholism  
  - Liver disease  
  - Hypothyroidism  
  - Myelodysplastic syndromes

**Lifestyle Recommendations:**  
- Maintain a balanced diet with iron, B12, and folate.  
- Limit alcohol consumption.  
- Regular screening if family history of anemia or thalassemia.  
- Manage chronic conditions (kidney, thyroid, liver disease).  
- Consult a doctor before self-supplementing vitamins.

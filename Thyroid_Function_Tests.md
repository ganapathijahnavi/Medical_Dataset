# Thyroid Function Tests (TSH, T3, T4)

**Normal Range:**  
- TSH: 0.4 – 4.0 mIU/L  
- T3: 80 – 180 ng/dL  
- T4: 4.5 – 12.5 mcg/dL  

---

**Clinical Significance:**  
Thyroid hormones regulate **metabolism, growth, and energy balance**. Abnormalities suggest **hypothyroidism** (underactive thyroid) or **hyperthyroidism** (overactive thyroid).  
- **High TSH + Low T3/T4 → Hypothyroidism**  
- **Low TSH + High T3/T4 → Hyperthyroidism**  
- **Normal T3/T4 but abnormal TSH → Subclinical thyroid disease**  

---

**Future Diseases:**  
- Hypothyroidism (Hashimoto’s, iodine deficiency, post-thyroidectomy)  
- Hyperthyroidism (Graves’ disease, toxic nodular goiter)  
- Thyroid cancer (detected with further imaging and biopsy)  

---

**Lifestyle Recommendations:**  
- Follow prescribed thyroid medication (levothyroxine, antithyroid drugs)  
- Ensure adequate iodine intake (but avoid excess)  
- Regular monitoring of thyroid function  
- Balanced diet and stress management
